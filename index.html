Living in the City

1 Living in the city can be exciting, 2 Last week, something happened that really took me aback. 3 I was walking in the park in the morning when a strange man came over and asked me for directions. 4 He looked friendly, so I took him aside to explain the way.

emocionante - exciting extraño - strange

5 I often come by new people on my way to work, and sometimes it is interesting. 6 For example, yesterday, I came by a small café near my apartment, and I decided to try their coffee. 7 The taste took me back to my hometown, and I felt happy.

probar - try sabor - taste ciudad natal - hometown

8 My apartment is small, but it is comfortable. 9 Sometimes, loud music comes from the apartments next door, and it takes away my peace. 10 But I try to ignore it.

ruidoso - loud al lado - next door

11 In the evening, friends often come in to visit me. 12 We talk about our day, and sometimes new problems come up at work. 13 Last week, one friend came out of nowhere with a funny story, and it really took us all aback.

de la nada - nowhere

14 I like living in the city because things are close. 15 Shops, cinemas, and buses are always near, and people always come through when you need help. 16 Even if life is busy, you learn to enjoy every moment in the city center or at the park nearby.

autobús - bus ocupado - busy cada - every
